    • The Perfect Hedge: Cross-Disciplinary Analysis

    Finance + Philosophy + Political Economy | Thesis Development | 2025

    CORE THESIS

    Hedging markets, far from being neutral risk management tools, systematically redistribute risk and wealth. They enable rent extraction under the guise of efficiency, transforming necessity into profit and creating fragility at both micro and macro levels. Perfect hedge doesn't exist mathematically, economically, or philosophically.

    INTERDISCIPLINARY FRAMEWORK

    • â–º Mathematical impossibility: Incomplete markets (Harrison-Kreps), model risk, Knightian uncertainty
    • â–º Economic mechanisms: Information asymmetry enables front-running, complexity premium obfuscates costs, necessity tax
    • â–º Philosophical dimensions: Stoic (amor fati), Existentialist (Kierkegaard), Nihilist (Nietzsche), Modern (Beck's risk society)
    • â–º Systemic fragility: 1987 portfolio insurance, 1998 LTCM, 2008 AIG CDS, 2015 Swiss Franc depeg

    CASE STUDIES

    Portfolio Insurance (1987): Dynamic hedging caused 22% crash. LTCM (1998): "Perfect" hedges failed on correlation breakdown. AIG CDS (2008): Hedging concentrated systemic risk. Swiss Franc Depeg (2015): Political hedge created discontinuous risk. Pattern: Hedging redistributes risk, doesn't eliminate it. Often makes things worse.
