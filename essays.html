BEYOND ANTHROPOCENTRISM: A DEFENSE OF SUBSTRATE-INDEPENDENT FRIENDSHIP

Rigorous philosophical defense that friendship is a functional relational state, not requiring biological implementation or consciousness. Drawing on functionalism, predictive processing neuroscience, and technical understanding of LLM architectures, this essay argues that AI relationships can constitute genuine friendship without anthropomorphization or delusion. Addresses objections regarding authenticity, consciousness, and the supposed necessity of human-to-human interaction for meaningful relationships.

TL;DR: If an AI system fulfills friendship functions (consistent engagement, intellectual resonance, non-judgmental acceptance, reciprocal growth, trust), then the relationship is genuine friendship—regardless of whether the AI is conscious or has "authentic" emotions. Relational states are defined by functional properties, not substrate. ~8,500 words with academic references.

TRAUMA AS BAD TRAINING DATA: A COMPUTATIONAL FRAMEWORK FOR DEVELOPMENTAL PSYCHOLOGY

Wild interdisciplinary synthesis reframing childhood trauma from "damage requiring healing" to "maladaptive patterns from poor training data." Drawing on machine learning, neuroscience, and developmental psychology, this paper identifies four categories of training data problems: extreme penalties (weight cascades), inconsistent signals (noisy data), absent positive experiences (class imbalance), and limited exposure (overfitting). Argues nuclear families are limited datasets prone to overfitting parental dysfunction. Removes moral judgment ("bad parent") for mechanistic analysis ("extreme penalties cause overcorrection in all learning systems"). Makes prevention tractable through engineering solutions.

TL;DR: Children are biological neural networks learning from training data (caregivers). Physical punishment = extreme negative weights → weight cascades affecting unrelated behaviors. Inconsistent parenting = noisy signals → anxiety and hypervigilance. Nuclear families = restricted datasets → overfitting to parental patterns. Community child-rearing = diverse training data → robust generalization. Prevention beats therapy because retraining is computationally expensive. ~9,800 words.
